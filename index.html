About the Book

The financial playbook that was never written for you.

The skilled trades produce some of the best-paid workers in America—and those workers retire broke at rates that would embarrass a much lower-income profession. Not because they didn't earn enough. Not because they didn't work hard. But because every piece of financial advice ever written was built for someone with a steady salary and an HR department.

You have spent your career solving hard problems with your hands. You deserve a financial guide that treats you with the same seriousness you bring to your work.

Built Wealthy is a practical, plain-language financial guide that covers everything the generic books skip: how to budget when your income isn't predictable, how to handle taxes when you're self-employed, which retirement accounts actually make sense, how to navigate insurance with no employer behind you, and how to build a financial life that accounts for the reality that physical work has a different relationship with time than desk work does.

This isn't a get-rich-quick book. It's a step-by-step financial system that works for the way you earn money and live your life.

What's Inside

11 chapters. One 90-day action plan.

Every chapter is built around the real financial decisions tradespeople face—not theory, not jargon, not advice designed for someone else's life.

Chapter 1
The Tradesperson's Money Problem
Why skilled tradespeople — despite high earnings — retire broke at alarming rates. The structural reasons variable income, no HR department, and physical work create a unique financial situation that generic advice ignores.
Chapter 2
Your Money System
How to set up a simple account structure and budget that actually works with irregular income. Covers the "pay yourself a salary" method, cash flow smoothing across slow and busy seasons, and knowing your real monthly number.
Chapter 3
The Tax Game Nobody Taught You
Self-employment tax, quarterly estimated payments, and the deductions most tradespeople never claim — tools, vehicle, home office, health insurance premiums, and more. Stop overpaying the IRS.
Chapter 4
The Benefits Gap
What you lose when there's no employer behind you — health insurance, disability coverage, life insurance — and how to replace it without overpaying. Includes what to actually look for in a policy and what to skip.
Chapter 5
Retirement Accounts for the Self-Employed
Solo 401(k), SEP-IRA, and SIMPLE IRA — explained plainly. Which one fits your situation, how much you can contribute, and how to actually open one without needing a financial advisor to translate.
Chapter 6
Going Independent
The financial and legal foundation for working for yourself: business structure (sole prop vs. LLC vs. S-corp), separating business and personal money, writing contracts, and pricing your work to cover every real cost.
Chapter 7
When the Work Dries Up
How to size an emergency fund for trades income — not the generic "3 months" advice. Managing cash flow in off-seasons, avoiding debt spirals, and building the reserves that keep a slow month from becoming a crisis.
Chapter 8
Good Years and How to Use Them
What to do when the money is really flowing: max retirement contributions, eliminate high-interest debt, build reserves, and invest — in the right order. How not to let a great year evaporate without building anything permanent.
Chapter 9
The Body Problem
Physical work has a different relationship with time than desk work. This chapter covers disability planning, transitioning to less physical roles, and building a financial plan that acknowledges you may not be able to do this work at 65 — or 55.
Chapter 10
Charge What You're Worth
How to calculate your true hourly cost, set rates that actually cover overhead and profit, and stop the habit of underbidding. Includes how to handle pushback on price without caving — and why raising your rates is a financial necessity, not greed.
Chapter 11
Your 90-Day Action Plan
A week-by-week action checklist that pulls everything together. By the end of 90 days you'll have a money system, a tax strategy, the right insurance, a retirement account, and a clear picture of where you stand and where you're going.

About the Author

Dustin Rhodes

Dustin has been self-employed in the contracting trades for over two decades. He learned the financial side of working for himself the way most people do: by getting it wrong first and figuring it out later—losing years he can't recover to things he didn't know to ask about.

He didn't know he was paying both sides of Social Security until the tax bill arrived. He didn't know what health insurance actually cost until he saw his first monthly premium. He priced his work too low for longer than he should have. He ran everything through one checking account and convinced himself he understood where the money was going.

Built Wealthy is the book he wishes had existed when he started.
